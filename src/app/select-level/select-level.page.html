Kegel exercises (also called pelvic floor exercises) help strengthen your pelvic floor muscles. Your pelvic floor muscles support organs in your pelvis, like your bladder, bowel, and vagina.

The pelvic floor muscles hold your organs in place while also assisting with bodily functions like peeing, pooping, and sex.

KEGEL EXERCISES AID WITH

  1. Urinary incontinence
    (leaking-pee)
  2. Urge incontinence
    (an urgent need to pee),
  3. Fecal incontinence
    (leaking-poop),
  4. Pelvic organ prolapse
    (pelvic-organs-sagging).

Kegel exercises help keep your pelvic floor muscles “fit.” Much like you may strengthen other muscles in your body by lifting weights, doing Kegels is a way to keep your pelvic floor muscles strong.

Kegels can also improve your sexual health and help improve your orgasms.

IMPORTANCE FOR FEMALES

Helps improve bladder control, especially after childbirth or with aging.

May enhance sexual pleasure and reduce the risk of pelvic organ prolapse.

Can be beneficial during and after pregnancy to support the pelvic organs and aid in recovery.

IMPORTANCE FOR MALES

Can improve bladder control and help with urinary incontinence.

May aid in erectile dysfunction by improving blood flow to the pelvic region.

Can benefit men after prostate surgery to regain bladder control and maintain pelvic floor health.
